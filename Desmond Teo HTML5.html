About Desmond

Desmond's training comes from both psychology as well as human capital management. Aside from work, his personal interest in Data Science and Internet of Things (IoT) often makes him think how the future of work would look like. Can AI (Artificial Intelligence) rule the world? It is up to human to learn, reflect and figure out!

Everyone is unique and Desmond believes career means more than merely securing a job. It is a process where individuals figure out what aligns them with their passion and aspirations - and that can change over time.

Desmond’s career

Desmond’s career started from the healthcare industry where he was heading healthcare operations, as well as driving talent acquisition for local and overseas campus recruitment.

Thereafter, he joined the public sector as a career coach during the 2008-2009 global financial crisis, in hope of assisting his fellow Singaporeans during tough times. Through his coaching work at the government agency, he was fortunate to have coached over 500 hundred professionals, managers, engineers, and teachers who walked through his door and assist them out of retrenchment and to make their mid-career switch decisions.


After serving in public service, Desmond headed STEM Career Advisory at NUS where he was responsible for strategy formulation as well as leading 10 career development managers across the STEM faculties (i.e. Faculty of Engineering, Faculty of Science & School of Computing), in driving career services for over 13,000 students.


As part of the role, Desmond worked with several government agencies and top multinational companies in advocating STEM careers. At present, Desmond manages careers and industry partnerships for the NUS Faculty of Engineering.
